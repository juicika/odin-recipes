Shakshuka

Description

Shakshuka is a simple and quick North African dish of eggs poached in a spicy stew of tomatoes and pappers that packs a punch. While this is a fairly straightforward recipe, you can add other sorts of things like sausage, kielbasa, feta, or parmesan cheese.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 2 onions, chopped (about 3 cups)
  • 4 red or green bell peppers, chopped
  • 6 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 2 tbsp grated dried lime, optional
  • 6 tbsp sweet paprika
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 8 cups tomato puree
  • 1 tbsp plus 1 tsp sugar
  • 16 large eggs

Steps

  1. Heat 1/4 cup of the oil over medium heat in a cast iron skillet large enough to accommodate 16 poached eggs. If you don't have a skillet that large, use two pans and divide the ingredients evenly between them.
  2. Add the onions, bell peppers, garlic, dried lime (if using), paprika, cumin, coriander, and salt and cook. Stir occasionally, until vegetables have softened but not browned, about 10 minutes.
  3. Add the tomato puree and sugar and simmer until reduced by about one-third, about 10 to 12 minutes.
  4. Whisk in remaining 1/4 cup of oil.
  5. Crack the eggs into the skillet, spacing them evenly in the sauce. Lower the heat, cover, and cook until the egg whites are set but the yolks remain runny, about 5 minutes.
  6. Top with serrano chiles and cilantro and serve immediately right from the pan.
